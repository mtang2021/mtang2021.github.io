Introduction and Background

The stock market reflects a country's economic health and provides valuable insights into its future. While it's impossible to predict a stock's exact movement with absolute certainty, we can reduce risk and forecast potential stock movements by analyzing key indicators such as market sentiment, GDP, and earnings reports.

Due to the time-consuming process of analyzing data, algorithmic trading has emerged as an effective measure to analyze the market more safely and efficiently. For this project, We will use FRED economic data, which contains U.S. macroeconomic data, Yahoo Finance, which contains useful APIs for daily market coverage, and FINRA's datasets to develop a profitable investing strategy that helps us predict the direction of the stock market.

Problem Definition

Predicting the direction of a stock is challenging due to its volatility. Analyzing data from various key indicators for a stock is also time-consuming. To streamline this process and improve market predictions, we will use machine learning models to forecast stock trends and maximize profit. By leveraging historical data and advanced algorithms, these models can identify patterns that may be difficult for humans to detect manually.

Methods

We anticipate that the data might have imperfections, which we aim to correct with these techniques:

  • For columns with missing data we will use k-nearest neighbors to see how similar data would behave, and based on that substitute the value
  • To reduce the number of features, and make the model simpler, we will use Principal Component Analysis (PCA). This will allow us to drop features while keeping the variance.
  • We will drop the outliers to prevent it from getting thrown off by data that is not representative, to do this we aim to remove the data below and above 2 x IQR (inter quartile range).

For our stock market predictor, we will use models such as Support Vector Machine (SVM), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and Linear regression, but for this project, performance depends mostly on how well we select the data.

  • SVM is a model that is used for instantaneously changing price predictions in complex stock markets because it has a high success rate in using high-dimensional data and classifying complex data. The model is also used to predict price movements.
  • CNN is traditionally used for graphic and template data. When we want to create an image, past price data is used as a 2-dimensional system in stock market prediction. CNN is effective in revealing subtle patterns in stock market data with its many technical indicators. The difference from SVM is that although both work with large data, CNN helps us visually see non-linear relationships.
  • Linear Regression is one of the simplest models used in stock market prediction. The most efficiency is obtained when the relationship between entry characteristics and exit characteristics is linear. While entry characteristics represent characteristics such as past prices, volume, exit characteristics can be thought of as the expected result and the estimated price that may exist in the future. The difference from the other 2 models is that it can be interpreted quickly in linear data that is not too complicated, but as the data becomes more complex, it may be more effective to use other models.

Potential Results & Discussion

To evaluate our model, we will be using metrics from our scikit-learn which include Mean-Squared-Error (MSE) which measures the average squared difference between predicted price and actual price, Mean-Absolute-Error (MAE) which measures average error in prediction and (R)^2 Score which measures how well unseen samples are likely to be predicted by the model. Through these metrics, our project goal is to successfully develop a stock predictive model that accurately forecasts prices of any stock using real-time data with a success rate of 80%. With a MSE of less than 5 dollars, MAE of less than 3 dollars and R^2 of greater than 0.8.
